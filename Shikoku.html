Top 5 Tourist Attractions

  • Ritsurin Garden: Ritsurin Garden (栗林公園) is a famous Japanese garden located in Takamatsu City, Kagawa Prefecture, on the island of Shikoku. It is widely regarded as one of Japan’s most beautiful and historically significant landscape gardens. The garden, designated a Special Place of Scenic Beauty, is a prime example of the "chinese-style" garden design that has been adapted to fit Japanese aesthetics.
  • Matsuyama Castle: Matsuyama Castle (松山城) is a historic Japanese castle located in Matsuyama City, the capital of Ehime Prefecture on the island of Shikoku. It is one of the most famous castles in Japan, known for its well-preserved structure, stunning views, and strategic location. Matsuyama Castle is considered an important cultural and historical symbol of the city and the region.
  • Iya Valley: Iya Valley (祖谷渓, Iya-kei) is a remote and picturesque region located in the mountainous interior of Shikoku Island, Tokushima Prefecture, Japan. Known for its dramatic gorges, steep cliffs, and historic vine bridges, Iya Valley is one of Japan's hidden gems, offering stunning natural beauty and a chance to experience rural Japanese culture. The valley is part of the Iya Valley National Park and is renowned for its unspoiled landscapes and traditional village life.
  • Kochi Castle: Kochi Castle (高知城, Kōchi-jō) is a historic Japanese castle located in Kochi City, the capital of Kochi Prefecture on Shikoku Island. It is one of Japan’s most well-preserved original castles, offering a glimpse into the country’s feudal history and samurai culture. The castle is notable for its beautiful location, traditional architecture, and historical significance.
  • Shikoku Pilgrimage: The Shikoku Pilgrimage (四国遍路, Shikoku Henro) is a spiritual journey that follows a circular route around the island of Shikoku, Japan. The pilgrimage is traditionally associated with the Buddhist monk Kōbō Daishi (also known as Kōbō Daishi or Kōbō Daishi, the founder of the Shingon Buddhist sect), and it involves visiting 88 temples that are spread across the four prefectures of Shikoku: Tokushima, Kagawa, Ehime, and Kochi.
