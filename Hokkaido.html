Top 5 Tourist Attractions

  • Sapporo Snow Festival: It is one of Japan's most famous winter events, held annually in early February in Sapporo, Hokkaido. The festival is renowned for its stunning snow and ice sculptures, ranging from intricate carvings of famous landmarks and cultural icons to massive creations that can be over 15 meters tall and 25 meters wide.
  • Hakodate: Hakodate (函館) is a picturesque port city located on the southern tip of Hokkaido, Japan's northernmost island. Known for its stunning night views, rich history, and fresh seafood, Hakodate offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural charm.
  • Furano and Biei: Furano and Biei are two picturesque towns located in the heart of Hokkaido, Japan, known for their stunning landscapes, seasonal beauty, and agricultural charm. They are popular destinations, especially during summer and winter.
  • Shiretoko National Park: Shiretoko National Park (知床国立公園) is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located on the northeastern tip of Hokkaido, Japan. It is one of the most remote and untouched areas of Japan, celebrated for its stunning natural landscapes, diverse wildlife, and opportunities for eco-tourism.
  • Noboribetsu Onsen: Noboribetsu Onsen (登別温泉) is one of Japan's most famous hot spring resorts, located in Hokkaido. Renowned for its variety of mineral-rich thermal waters and dramatic volcanic landscapes, it’s a haven for relaxation and natural beauty.
