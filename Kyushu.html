Top 5 Tourist Attractions

  • Mount Aso: Mount Aso (阿蘇山, Aso-san) is an active volcano and the centerpiece of Aso Caldera, located in the Kumamoto Prefecture on the island of Kyushu, Japan. It is one of the largest volcanoes in the world in terms of caldera size, and its central crater contains a highly active volcanic system, making it a fascinating and popular destination for visitors interested in geology, nature, and adventure.
  • Beppu Onsen: Beppu Onsen (別府温泉) is one of Japan’s most famous hot spring resorts, located in Beppu City, Oita Prefecture, on the island of Kyushu. It is renowned for its abundance of hot springs, with over 2,000 hot spring sources making it one of the largest and most diverse onsen (hot spring) areas in the country. Beppu offers visitors a variety of onsen experiences, from traditional baths to unique geothermal features, and it’s a popular destination for those seeking relaxation and wellness in the midst of beautiful natural surroundings.
  • Kumamoto Castle: Kumamoto Castle (熊本城, Kumamoto-jō) is one of Japan’s most famous and historically significant castles, located in Kumamoto City, Kumamoto Prefecture, on the island of Kyushu. Known for its impressive architecture, strong defensive features, and rich history, Kumamoto Castle is often considered one of Japan's three great castles, along with Himeji Castle and Matsumoto Castle. The castle is a prime example of Japanese feudal-era military architecture and remains a symbol of Kumamoto’s heritage.
  • Nagasaki Peace Park: Nagasaki Peace Park (長崎平和公園, Nagasaki Heiwa Kōen) is a significant memorial park located in Nagasaki City, Nagasaki Prefecture, on the island of Kyushu, Japan. Established to commemorate the victims of the atomic bombing of Nagasaki on August 9, 1945, during World War II, the park serves as a symbol of peace, hope, and the enduring desire to prevent future nuclear warfare. It is part of the larger Nagasaki Atomic Bomb Museum complex, dedicated to educating visitors about the devastation caused by the bomb and promoting peace efforts worldwide.
  • Yakushima Island: Yakushima Island (屋久島, Yakushima) is a remote island located off the southern coast of Kyushu in Japan, known for its rich biodiversity, ancient forests, and lush landscapes. It is part of Kagoshima Prefecture and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site recognized for its outstanding natural beauty. Yakushima is particularly famous for its ancient cedar trees, some of which are believed to be over 1,000 years old, and its unique ecosystems, which attract nature lovers, hikers, and those seeking tranquility in a pristine environment.
